ALAN TURING


  • In 1950, he published an article called 'Computing machinery and intelligence',
    that later became known as the Turing's test, in which he explained the idea that it was possible
    to reach artificial intelligence following only the patterns of the human mind.
  • In 1951, he was elected member of the Royal Society and he went to the University of Manchester,
    where he built the Manchester Automatic Digital Machine (MADM), creating algorithms and programs,
    and taking part to the writing of the operating manual.
    MADM soon became one of the most used computers with stored program.
  • In 1952 Turing was arrested for homosexual acts and he had two choices,
    either staying in prison for two years or undergoing a chemical castration treatment and he chose the last one.
  • On 7 June 1954, he committed suicide eating an apple which he had poisoned with cyanide,
    like in the fairy tale of Snow White and the seven dwarfs.
